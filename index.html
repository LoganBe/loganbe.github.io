About Logan

Logan received his bachelors in science at Stony Brook University in 2017 for both Physics and Astronomy. Afterwards he continued his education at Stony Brook where he obtained his Masters in Neuroscience and Behavior in 2019 while working under Dr. Memming Park. During and after his Masters, Logan teched at Dr. Mathew Wilsons lab at MIT, working under Dr. Carmen Varela through both MIT and Florida Atlantic University. Currently Logan is enrolled as a PhD student at the University of Texas at Austin where he is working under Dr. Thibaud Taillefumier.

Research Interest

Logans scientific interest is focused around understanding neural dynamics through the biophysically inspired models. His work utilizes techniques combining neuroscience data with neural dynamic theories, computational modeling, machine learning and a with a strong focus of neural statistics and point processes. His current work focuses on the development of neural variability, or the source of the fluctuations of the membrane potential recorded from in vivo, awake behaving Macaque V1.

Outreach

Climate change, COVID, and even flat earth, these are just some exampels where there is rapid science dis- and misinformation across the country. In order to address this issue and restore trust in science and scientests alike, it is crucial to open a dialogue between people of all ages to provide answers and comfort regarding the current scientific results of today. Logan strives to push towards clear communication by opening outreach programs with the goals to: Spread awareness of the scientific process and how science is conducted in todays labs, provide sources of information for all people to obtain scientific information, and educate children of all ages about all breathes of science.
